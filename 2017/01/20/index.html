Friday, January 20, 2017

Donald J. Trump's Day: Presidential Inauguration

A chronological account of executive actions, public appearances, and notable developments

Washington, D.C. — Donald John Trump was sworn in as the 45th President of the United States before an estimated 300,000 to 600,000 attendees at the U.S. Capitol, delivering a populist inaugural address that promised to end "American carnage".

Daily Chronology

8:30 AM: Trump attended church service at Saint John's Church across from the White House with family, VP-elect Pence, and Cabinet designees.
9:30 AM: White House tea reception hosted by President Obama and First Lady Michelle Obama at the South Portico.
10:30 AM: Trump and family departed for the U.S. Capitol for swearing-in ceremony.
12:00 PM: Trump and Pence sworn in at the West Front of the U.S. Capitol by Chief Justice John Roberts, followed by inaugural address.
1:00 PM: Joint Congressional inaugural luncheon at the U.S. Capitol.
2:30 PM: Military review ceremony at the East Front of the U.S. Capitol.
3:00 PM: Inaugural parade viewed from stands outside the White House.
7:00 PM: Liberty and Freedom Ball at Washington Convention Center, followed by Military Ball at National Building Museum.

Executive Actions

  • Took presidential oath of office administered by Chief Justice John Roberts. ("I, Donald John Trump, do solemnly swear that I will faithfully execute the office of President of the United States...")
  • Delivered inaugural address outlining "America First" policy vision and domestic priorities.
"This American carnage stops right here and stops right now."

Press Coverage

  • TIME Magazine: Described the speech as "rancorous" and noted Trump's announcement of a "sharp U-turn in U.S. policy" with "America First" theme.
  • CNN: Provided extensive live coverage of the ceremony and noted the peaceful transfer of power from the Obama administration.

Historical Significance

January 20, 2017 marked the 58th presidential inauguration in U.S. history and completed the peaceful transfer of power from Barack Obama to Donald Trump. The ceremony represented the culmination of Trump's extraordinary rise from businessman and reality TV star to President, following a tumultuous 2016 election campaign. Trump's inaugural address broke with traditional conciliatory tones, instead painting a stark picture of American decline and promising dramatic policy changes under an "America First" doctrine. The speech's reference to "American carnage" and its populist themes signaled a departure from conventional presidential rhetoric and foreshadowed the unconventional presidency that would follow.
