Monday, January 23, 2017

Donald J. Trump's Day: TPP Withdrawal and Business Outreach

A chronological account of executive actions, public appearances, and notable developments

Washington, D.C. - President Trump formally withdrew the United States from the Trans-Pacific Partnership trade agreement and met with business leaders in the Roosevelt Room to discuss his "America First" economic agenda.

Daily Chronology

9:30 AM: President Trump received his daily intelligence briefing from CIA Director John Brennan and national security staff in the Oval Office (WH Pool).
11:45 AM: Met with senior White House staff including Chief of Staff Reince Priebus, Steve Bannon, and Jared Kushner to review weekly agenda.
12:30 PM: Hosted business leaders including CEOs from major corporations in the Roosevelt Room for roundtable discussion on trade and jobs.
2:00 PM: Signed Presidential Memorandum withdrawing the United States from the Trans-Pacific Partnership (TPP) in the Oval Office.
2:15 PM: Made brief remarks to press pool about TPP withdrawal, calling it "a great thing for the American worker."
4:30 PM: Participated in swearing-in ceremony for White House senior staff in the East Room.
Evening: Private dinner with family in White House residence while reviewing briefing materials for upcoming meetings.

Executive Actions

  • Signed Presidential Memorandum withdrawing the United States from the Trans-Pacific Partnership (TPP). ("The United States will not be parties to the Trans-Pacific Partnership")
  • Imposed federal hiring freeze through memorandum to all executive departments and agencies, exempting military personnel.
  • Directed review of all pending federal regulations from previous administration for potential withdrawal or modification.

Press Coverage

  • The Wall Street Journal: Analyzed the TPP withdrawal's impact on U.S. economic influence in Asia, noting concerns from business groups about lost opportunities (WSJ)
  • The New York Times: Reported on Asian allies' disappointment with the decision and potential benefits to China's regional trade influence (NYT)
  • Fox Business: Praised Trump's fulfillment of campaign promises on trade and highlighted support from labor unions for the TPP withdrawal
  • CNN: Covered the business leaders meeting, noting mixed reactions from corporate executives about new trade policies
  • Politico: Detailed the federal hiring freeze and its potential impact on government operations and services (Politico)

Historical Significance

The withdrawal from TPP marked Trump's first major foreign policy decision and represented a fundamental shift in U.S. trade strategy after decades of pursuing multilateral trade agreements. The 12-nation TPP, negotiated under President Obama, was designed to counter China's growing economic influence in the Asia-Pacific region by creating American-led trade standards. Trump's withdrawal effectively ceded this strategic advantage to China and marked the beginning of his "America First" trade policy.

The federal hiring freeze, while popular with Trump's base, created immediate practical challenges for government agencies and signaled a broader effort to reduce federal workforce size. This day established the administration's approach of using executive actions to quickly reverse Obama-era policies, a strategy that would define much of Trump's first months in office. The business leader meetings also began what would become a pattern of direct corporate engagement to promote domestic job creation and manufacturing investment (Various sources).
