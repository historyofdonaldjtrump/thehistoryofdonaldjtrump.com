Saturday, January 28, 2017

Donald J. Trump's Day: Airport Protests and Putin Call

A chronological account of executive actions, public appearances, and notable developments
Advertisement

WASHINGTON/NATIONWIDE - Mass protests erupted at airports across America as Trump's travel ban took effect, while the President conducted his first phone call with Russian President Vladimir Putin in the Oval Office (NPR, Wikipedia).

Daily Chronology

11:00 AM: First protesters gather at JFK Airport Terminal 4 as approximately 30 people begin demonstrating against travel ban detentions (Wikipedia).
Afternoon: Protests spread to over 30 airports nationwide including O'Hare, LAX, SFO, BWI, and Sea-Tac as crowds grow into the thousands (Wikipedia).
Evening: Trump conducts one-hour phone call with Russian President Vladimir Putin from the Oval Office, joined by senior staff including Flynn, Pence, and Bannon (NPR).
6:00-7:00 PM: New York Taxi Workers Alliance stages one-hour strike, refusing to pick up passengers at JFK Airport in solidarity with detainees (Wikipedia).
9:37 PM: Federal Judge Ann Donnelly in Brooklyn issues emergency stay blocking parts of the travel ban, providing relief to stranded travelers (Wikipedia).

Executive Actions

  • Signed memorandum directing Pentagon to develop plan for defeating ISIS within 30 days, emphasizing new coalition partners (NPR).
  • Travel ban enforcement continues at airports nationwide with approximately 27 passengers detained or sent home on first day (Wikipedia).

Press Coverage

  • The New York Times: Focused on the spontaneous nature of airport protests and legal challenges mounting against the travel ban (Read more)
  • NPR: Emphasized the Putin phone call's focus on ISIS cooperation while noting sanctions were not discussed
  • CNN: Reported on the chaos at airports with hundreds of protesters and dozens of detainees nationwide
  • Fox News: Highlighted the president's determination to implement security measures despite protests

Historical Significance

January 28, 2017, marked the first major resistance to Trump's presidency as his travel ban sparked the largest spontaneous protests since his inauguration. The demonstrations at over 30 airports nationwide, mobilized through social media, demonstrated the power of grassroots organizing in the digital age and established a template for future resistance movements (Wikipedia).

The day was equally significant for Trump's foreign policy as his hour-long phone call with Putin represented the administration's first major diplomatic outreach to Russia. With National Security Advisor Michael Flynn present, the conversation focused on ISIS cooperation while avoiding contentious issues like sanctions and Ukraine, setting the tone for Trump's controversial approach to US-Russia relations that would dominate his presidency (NPR).
