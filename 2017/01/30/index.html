Monday, January 30, 2017

Donald J. Trump's Day: The Monday Night Massacre

A chronological account of executive actions, public appearances, and notable developments
Advertisement

WASHINGTON - President Trump fired Acting Attorney General Sally Yates after she refused to defend his travel ban, accusing her of betraying the Department of Justice in a dramatic showdown that echoed the Nixon era (CNN).

Daily Chronology

Day: Legal challenges continue mounting against the travel ban with cases filed in at least five states including Virginia, New York, Massachusetts, Washington and California (CNN).
Evening: CNN reports that Yates has instructed Justice Department lawyers not to defend Trump's executive order, calling it potentially unlawful (CNN).
8:30 PM: Trump tweets about Democratic obstruction, saying "Now have an Obama A.G." just hours before firing Yates (CNN).
9:00 PM: White House fires Yates via hand-delivered letter, immediately swearing in Dana Boente as acting Attorney General (CNN).
Late evening: Boente rescinds Yates' order and instructs DOJ lawyers to "defend the lawful orders of our President" (CNN).

Executive Actions

  • Fired Acting Attorney General Sally Yates for refusing to defend the travel ban executive order (CNN).
  • Appointed Dana Boente, US Attorney for Eastern District of Virginia, as acting Attorney General (CNN).
  • New acting AG immediately reversed Yates' directive and ordered Justice Department to defend travel ban (CNN).

  • NBC News: Focused on Yates being an Obama holdover and her concerns about the order's legality
  • Time Magazine: Covered the swift replacement with Dana Boente and the immediate policy reversal

Historical Significance

January 30, 2017, became known as the "Monday Night Massacre" for its echoes of Nixon's Saturday Night Massacre during Watergate. Trump's firing of Yates marked the first time since Nixon that a president had dismissed a top Justice Department official for refusing to carry out presidential orders. The drama illustrated the constitutional tension between executive power and prosecutorial independence that would define much of Trump's presidency (CNN).

The swift appointment and reversal by Dana Boente demonstrated Trump's determination to enforce his travel ban despite mounting legal challenges. With cases pending in five states and growing protests nationwide, the firing effectively ended the Justice Department's brief resistance to the executive order. However, it also galvanized opposition and gave Democrats a powerful symbol of principled resistance, with former Attorney General Loretta Lynch praising Yates' "courageous" leadership (CNN).
