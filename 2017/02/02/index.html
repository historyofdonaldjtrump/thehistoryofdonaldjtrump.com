Thursday, February 2, 2017

Donald J. Trump's Day: Prayer Breakfast and Policy

A chronological account of executive actions, public appearances, and notable developments
Advertisement

WASHINGTON - President Trump spoke at the National Prayer Breakfast, met with Congressional tax leaders, had lunch with Harley-Davidson executives, and addressed Supreme Court confirmation strategy while cabinet nominations advanced through Senate committees (CBS News).

Daily Chronology

Morning: Trump addressed the National Prayer Breakfast, making remarks about television ratings for "The Apprentice" and urging attendees to pray for Arnold Schwarzenegger (CBS News).
11:15 AM: Met with Senate Finance Committee Chairman Orrin Hatch, ranking Democrat Ron Wyden, House Ways and Means Chair Kevin Brady, and ranking member Richard Neal to discuss tax policy (CBS News).
Lunch: Hosted Harley-Davidson executives at the White House for discussions on manufacturing and trade policy (CBS News).
Afternoon: Advised Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell to "go nuclear" if needed to confirm Supreme Court nominee Neil Gorsuch (CBS News).

Executive Actions

  • Treasury Department issued new guidance allowing certain transactions between U.S. companies and Russia's FSB security service under modified sanctions (CBS News).
  • White House issued clarified guidance formally exempting Green Card holders from the travel ban executive order (CBS News).
  • National Security Adviser Michael Flynn put Iran "on notice" following ballistic missile test and Houthi rebel attacks (CBS News).

Historical Significance

February 2, 2017, demonstrated Trump's unconventional approach to presidential traditions, as his National Prayer Breakfast remarks about television ratings marked a departure from the typically solemn tone of the annual interfaith gathering. His willingness to discuss entertainment industry matters at a religious event illustrated his comfort with blending his celebrity background with presidential duties (CBS News).

The day also highlighted Trump's hands-on approach to congressional strategy, particularly his direct advice to McConnell about using the "nuclear option" for Supreme Court confirmations. This represented a significant shift from traditional presidential deference to Senate procedures and foreshadowed future battles over judicial nominees. Meanwhile, the Treasury Department's modification of Russia sanctions and clarification of travel ban exemptions showed the administration working to refine early policy implementations amid ongoing controversies (CBS News).
