Thursday, February 9, 2017

Donald J. Trump's Day: Jeff Sessions Sworn In as Attorney General

A chronological account of executive actions, public appearances, and notable developments
Advertisement

Washington - President Trump attended the swearing-in ceremony of Jeff Sessions as Attorney General following a contentious Senate confirmation process, while facing criticism from his own Supreme Court nominee Neil Gorsuch over attacks on the federal judiciary.

Daily Chronology

9:00 AM: Received daily intelligence briefing at the White House.
Morning: Attended swearing-in ceremony for Jeff Sessions as the 84th U.S. Attorney General.
12:30 PM: White House press briefing by Press Secretary Sean Spicer defending Yemen raid as "absolutely a success."
Afternoon: Northeast snowstorm preparations underway as major weather system approaches region.
Evening: 9th Circuit Court of Appeals upholds lower court ruling blocking travel ban executive order.

Cabinet Developments

  • Jeff Sessions sworn in: Trump personally attended the ceremony as Sessions became Attorney General following his 52-47 Senate confirmation vote after bitter partisan battle.
  • Elizabeth Warren aftermath: Continued fallout from Warren being silenced during Senate debate, with Republicans suggesting she orchestrated incident for 2020 presidential campaign visibility.
  • Double standard allegations: Democratic male senators later read same Coretta Scott King letter without punishment, raising questions about Rule XIX enforcement.

Supreme Court and Judicial Issues

  • Gorsuch criticizes Trump: Supreme Court nominee called Trump's verbal attacks on federal judiciary "demoralizing and disheartening" in private meeting with Democratic senator.
  • Legal community pushback: Appellate lawyers from across the country wrote formal letter to Justice Department complaining about presidential attacks on judicial branch.
  • Political calculation: Gorsuch's criticism viewed as strategic move to show independence and court Democratic votes needed to avoid filibuster.
  • Travel ban defeat: 9th Circuit Court of Appeals unanimously upheld lower court injunction blocking travel ban, dealing major legal blow to administration.

National Security Matters

  • Yemen raid defense: Press Secretary Spicer called January counter-terrorism raid "absolutely a success" and demanded critics like Sen. John McCain apologize for questioning operation.
  • Casualty controversy: Raid resulted in death of Navy SEAL Ryan Owens and at least 23 civilians, leading to criticism over mission planning and execution.
  • Yemen objects: Yemeni government announced it no longer wants U.S. conducting ground operations without prior approval following civilian casualties.
  • Venezuela passport investigation: CNN investigation revealed potential security threat from Venezuelan diplomatic mission allegedly selling passports to individuals with terrorism connections.

Travel Ban Court Ruling

  • Unanimous appeals court decision: 9th Circuit Court of Appeals denied Trump administration's appeal to reinstate travel ban, keeping lower court injunction in place.
  • Constitutional concerns: Court expressed skepticism about administration's national security arguments and due process violations.
  • Supreme Court path: Decision sets up potential Supreme Court review of executive power in immigration and national security matters.
  • Administration options: White House considering rewriting executive order versus appealing to Supreme Court.

Other Notable Events

  • Northeast blizzard: Major snowstorm bringing up to one foot of snow to New York, Philadelphia, and Boston, forcing widespread school closures and flight cancellations.
  • Archaeological discovery: First new Dead Sea Scrolls cave found in over 60 years, containing scroll storage jars and ancient artifacts.
  • Winter Olympics countdown: Exactly one year until 2018 Winter Olympics begin in Pyeongchang, South Korea.

Press Coverage

  • CNN: Featured Sessions swearing-in and Gorsuch criticism as lead stories in "5 Things" briefing, highlighting judicial independence themes.
  • Major Networks: Extensive coverage of travel ban court defeat and implications for presidential authority.
  • Legal Analysis: Constitutional scholars debated separation of powers and judicial review in immigration law.

Historical Significance

February 9, 2017 marked a defining moment in the early Trump presidency's relationship with the judicial branch. The unanimous 9th Circuit ruling against the travel ban represented the first major constitutional check on Trump's executive authority, while Neil Gorsuch's criticism of presidential attacks on judges highlighted tensions between executive power and judicial independence.

The day underscored the complex dynamics within Trump's own administration, as his Supreme Court nominee felt compelled to distance himself from the president's rhetoric to secure confirmation. The Sessions swearing-in, following the Elizabeth Warren silencing controversy, demonstrated how traditional governmental ceremonies were becoming flashpoints in an increasingly polarized political environment. These events foreshadowed the ongoing constitutional battles that would characterize Trump's presidency regarding executive power, judicial review, and institutional norms.
