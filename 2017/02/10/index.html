Friday, February 10, 2017

Donald J. Trump's Day: Flynn-Russia Reports and Kellyanne Conway Ethics Violation

A chronological account of executive actions, public appearances, and notable developments
Advertisement

Washington - President Trump faced multiple ethics controversies as reports emerged that National Security Adviser Michael Flynn discussed sanctions with the Russian ambassador before the inauguration, while White House counselor Kellyanne Conway violated federal ethics laws by promoting Ivanka Trump's merchandise on television.

Daily Chronology

9:00 AM: Received daily intelligence briefing with National Security Adviser Michael Flynn present.
11:00 AM: Joint press conference with Japanese Prime Minister Shinzo Abe at the White House.
12:30 PM: Signed executive orders on crime reduction and immigration enforcement.
Afternoon: Reports broke about Flynn's pre-inauguration conversations with Russian Ambassador Sergey Kislyak.
Evening: Told press pool he was unaware of Flynn-Russia sanctions discussions.

Michael Flynn Sanctions Controversy

  • Intelligence reports revealed: U.S. officials confirmed Flynn discussed sanctions with Russian Ambassador Sergey Kislyak on December 29, 2016, before Trump took office.
  • Flynn aide response: Flynn claimed he had "no recollection of discussing sanctions" and "couldn't be certain that the topic never came up."
  • Trump denies knowledge: President told reporters he was unaware of reports about Flynn's conversations with Russian officials.
  • Legal implications: Discussions raised questions about potential violations of the Logan Act, which prohibits private citizens from conducting foreign policy.

Kellyanne Conway Ethics Violation

  • TV endorsement: Conway used Fox & Friends appearance to explicitly promote Ivanka Trump's clothing line, stating she would "give a free commercial here."
  • Federal law violation: Conway's endorsement violated federal ethics regulations prohibiting government officials from using their positions to promote private businesses.
  • Republican criticism: House Oversight Committee Chairman Jason Chaffetz (R-Utah) called Conway's actions "absolutely wrong, wrong, wrong" and "over the top."
  • White House response: Administration said it had "counseled" Conway but declined to specify any disciplinary action.
  • Ethics review ordered: House Oversight Committee announced it would request Office of Government Ethics review and recommend discipline.

International Relations

  • Japan summit: Joint press conference with Prime Minister Shinzo Abe addressing trade, defense cooperation, and regional security challenges.
  • One China policy: Trump reportedly committed to maintaining U.S. "One China" policy in phone call with Chinese President Xi Jinping, reversing previous ambiguous statements.
  • Russia relations: Flynn controversy overshadowed diplomatic activities, raising questions about administration's Russia policy coordination.

Executive Orders and Policy

  • Crime reduction order: Signed executive order directing Attorney General to develop comprehensive plan to reduce violent crime in America.
  • Immigration enforcement: Additional executive orders strengthening immigration enforcement and interior deportation operations.
  • ICE raids controversy: Reports of Immigration and Customs Enforcement home raids in Los Angeles sparked protests and concerns about expanded deportation operations.
  • Arizona deportation case: Mother of two deported under new enforcement guidelines, highlighting policy implementation impacts.

Cabinet Confirmations

  • Tom Price confirmed: Senate confirmed Price as Health and Human Services Secretary, advancing Republican healthcare agenda.
  • Ongoing nominations: Several Cabinet positions remained pending Senate confirmation votes.
  • Ethics concerns: Conway violation added to growing list of ethics questions surrounding administration officials.

Ivanka Trump Business Disputes

  • Nordstrom tweet: Trump's official presidential Twitter account attacked Nordstrom for dropping Ivanka's clothing line, calling treatment "unfair" and "terrible."
  • Ethics concerns: Presidential endorsement of family business raised questions about conflicts of interest and abuse of office.
  • Conway follow-up: Conway's TV endorsement amplified controversy over using government positions to promote Trump family businesses.
  • Bipartisan criticism: Both Republican and Democratic lawmakers expressed concern about mixing official duties with family business interests.

Press Coverage

  • Democracy Now: Featured Conway ethics violation and Flynn-Russia reports as lead stories, emphasizing legal and constitutional implications.
  • CNN and major networks: Extensive coverage of Flynn sanctions discussions and potential Logan Act violations.
  • Washington Times: Detailed reporting on Flynn's conversations with Russian ambassador and timeline of events.
  • Ethics focus: Media coverage increasingly centered on administration ethics compliance and oversight mechanisms.

Historical Significance

February 10, 2017 marked a critical escalation in ethics controversies that would plague the Trump administration. The Flynn-Russia sanctions revelation represented the first major evidence of potential inappropriate foreign contacts by senior administration officials, foreshadowing the broader Russia investigation that would dominate much of Trump's presidency.

Conway's explicit endorsement of Ivanka Trump's products on television demonstrated how traditional ethical boundaries were being tested, with a senior White House official directly using her government position to promote the president's family business interests. The bipartisan criticism, including from Republican oversight chair Jason Chaffetz, showed that some ethical lines remained difficult to cross even in a polarized political environment. These incidents established patterns of controversy over conflicts of interest, foreign contacts, and ethical compliance that would become recurring themes throughout the Trump presidency.
