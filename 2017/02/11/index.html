Saturday, February 11, 2017

Donald J. Trump's Day: Golf Diplomacy with Japan's Abe at Mar-a-Lago

A chronological account of executive actions, public appearances, and notable developments
Advertisement

West Palm Beach, Florida - President Trump hosted Japanese Prime Minister Shinzo Abe for golf and diplomatic meetings at his Mar-a-Lago resort, while facing ongoing investigations into National Security Adviser Michael Flynn's pre-inauguration contacts with Russian officials and a Russian intelligence dossier gaining credibility with law enforcement.

Daily Chronology

Morning: Golf at Trump National Golf Club in Jupiter with Prime Minister Abe.
11:00 AM: First Lady Melania Trump took Akie Abe on tour of Morikami Museum and Gardens in Delray Beach.
12:00 PM: Bilateral meeting with Prime Minister Abe at Mar-a-Lago.
1:00 PM: Joint press conference with Prime Minister Abe.
Evening: Dinner with Japanese delegation at Mar-a-Lago resort.
Late Evening: Posted Twitter photo of golf course "high-five" with Abe.

Japan-U.S. Summit Activities

  • Golf diplomacy: Trump and Abe played golf at Trump National Golf Club in Jupiter on the 285-acre property, away from media coverage.
  • Bilateral meetings: Private discussions on trade, security cooperation, and regional challenges including North Korea.
  • Cultural exchange: First Ladies visited Morikami Museum and Gardens, showcasing Japanese-American cultural ties.
  • Social media diplomacy: Trump tweeted golf course photo with caption: "Having a great time hosting Prime Minister Shinzo Abe in the United States!"

Flynn-Russia Investigation Developments

  • Russian sanctions discussion confirmed: Multiple law enforcement sources confirmed Michael Flynn privately discussed U.S. sanctions against Russia with Russian Ambassador Sergey Kislyak before Trump took office.
  • Legal implications: Investigators believe Flynn's pre-inauguration call could constitute a violation of protocol and potentially violate federal law.
  • Russian dossier credibility: CBS News reported that the 35-page dossier compiled by former British intelligence officer Christopher Steele was gaining credibility among law enforcement agencies.
  • Multi-agency investigation: FBI leading investigation with involvement from CIA and NSA into whether Russia gathered compromising information on Trump during his business dealings.

Travel Ban and Immigration Policy

  • New executive order promised: Following 9th Circuit Court defeat, Trump indicated he would sign a new travel ban rather than continue appealing the original order.
  • Border wall cost controversy: Trump tweeted dismissively about reports that border wall construction could cost $21 billion, calling media coverage "fake news."
  • Legal system criticism: Trump tweeted that recent court decision blocking travel ban was "SO DANGEROUS" and criticized the "broken" legal system.
  • Timeline acceleration: Administration promised "something very rapidly" regarding additional security measures for the following week.

Israel Embassy Considerations

  • Jerusalem embassy decision: In interview with Israel Hayom, Trump said he was "thinking about it very seriously" regarding moving U.S. Embassy from Tel Aviv to Jerusalem.
  • Complexity acknowledged: Trump noted the embassy decision was "not easy" and had been avoided by previous administrations for "many, many years."
  • Regional implications: Potential embassy move could inflame tensions across Islamic world and complicate Middle East diplomacy.
  • Careful consideration: Trump emphasized he was "studying the embassy [issue]" rather than rushing to decision.

Administration Personnel

  • Tom Price confirmation: Senate confirmed Rep. Tom Price as Health and Human Services Secretary by 52-47 vote, advancing Republican healthcare agenda.
  • Upcoming confirmations: David Shulkin scheduled for Veterans Affairs Secretary vote, Linda McMahon for Small Business Administration.
  • Federal Reserve vacancy: Daniel Tarullo announced resignation from Fed Board, giving Trump third Fed board seat to fill and opportunity to roll back financial regulations.
  • Regulatory philosophy: Tarullo's departure cleared way for Trump to select candidate more aligned with Dodd-Frank rollback desires.

China Policy Clarification

  • "One China" policy reaffirmed: Trump spoke with Chinese President Xi Jinping by phone, confirming U.S. commitment to longstanding "One China" policy.
  • Diplomatic tensions eased: Call potentially alleviated concerns about major shift in Washington-Beijing relations following Trump's previous ambiguous statements.
  • Economic implications: Policy clarification important for trade relationships and regional stability in Asia-Pacific.
  • Strategic coordination: Phone call demonstrated administration's recognition of need for stable China relationship.

Press Coverage

  • CBS News: Comprehensive coverage of Japan summit and detailed reporting on Flynn-Russia investigation developments, emphasizing law enforcement source confirmations.
  • Media access limitations: Reporters unable to observe Trump-Abe golf activities on private resort property, relying on official photos.
  • White House Correspondents' Dinner: Major Garrett urged reporters not to boycott annual dinner despite tensions with Trump administration.
  • Investigation reporting: Focus on multi-agency nature of Russia investigation and growing credibility of intelligence dossier.

Historical Significance

February 11, 2017 demonstrated Trump's preference for conducting diplomacy at his private properties, establishing a pattern that would characterize his presidency. The Mar-a-Lago summit with Prime Minister Abe showcased "golf diplomacy" but also raised questions about security protocols and transparency when conducting official business at commercial properties.

The day marked a crucial escalation in the Flynn-Russia investigation, with law enforcement sources confirming that the National Security Adviser had indeed discussed sanctions with Russian officials before Trump took office. This development, combined with reports that the Steele dossier was gaining credibility among investigators, signaled that the Russia investigation was becoming a serious legal matter rather than merely political controversy. The convergence of these investigations with ongoing travel ban litigation demonstrated the multiple legal challenges facing the young administration.
