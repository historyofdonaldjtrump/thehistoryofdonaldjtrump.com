Washington, D.C. - President Trump fired Federal Reserve Governor Lisa Cook and signed a significant executive order creating specialized National Guard units for domestic law enforcement, escalating his battles with both the Federal Reserve and crime in major cities (CNN).

Daily Chronology

Day: President Trump signed executive order creating specialized National Guard units to "deal with public order issues," expanding military's domestic law enforcement role (CNN).
Day: Scheduled meeting with South Korean President as part of diplomatic agenda (Conservative Daily News).
Evening: President Trump announced the firing of Federal Reserve Governor Lisa Cook, significantly escalating his battle with the Fed over interest rate policies (CNN).

Executive Actions

  • Signed executive order "Taking Steps to End Cashless Bail to Protect Americans" (White House).
  • Created specialized National Guard units for domestic law enforcement and public order issues (CNN).
  • Issued proclamation marking the "Fourth Anniversary of the Attack at Abbey Gate, 2025" (White House).
  • Dismissed Federal Reserve Governor Lisa Cook from her position (CNN).

Historical Significance

August 25, 2025, marked one of the most consequential days in President Trump's second term domestic policy agenda. The firing of Federal Reserve Governor Lisa Cook represents an unprecedented escalation in presidential conflict with the Fed, which Trump has criticized for being too slow to lower interest rates. This action demonstrates the administration's willingness to challenge traditional boundaries between executive and monetary policy independence.

The signing of executive orders creating specialized National Guard units for domestic law enforcement represents a significant expansion of military involvement in civilian policing. This move, combined with the cashless bail order, signals a comprehensive approach to crime policy that extends federal authority into traditionally state and local jurisdictions. The day also included diplomatic engagement with South Korea and commemorated the fourth anniversary of the Abbey Gate attack, showing the breadth of presidential responsibilities even on days marked by major domestic policy shifts.
